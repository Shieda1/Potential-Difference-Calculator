What is the potential difference?
Potential difference is the difference in electric potential between two points. In a static electric field, it corresponds to the work needed per unit of charge to move a test charge between two points. It is also known as voltage or electric pressure.
How to calculate potential difference?
To calculate potential difference you should use the following formula:
V = I * R
where:
V = voltage
I = current
R = resistance
Why use potential difference?
The potential difference is used to compare points when figuring out how they will ineract with a single charge.
